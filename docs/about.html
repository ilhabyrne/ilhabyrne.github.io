About

I am currently a PhD candidate at the University of Queensland working on coral evolutionary and conservation genomics in the Riginos Research Group.

My PhD research looks at patterns of spatial genetic variation in corals on the Great Barrier Reef and in Honduras. My PhD is primarily funded by the Reef Restoration and Adaptation Program, the Paul M. Angell Foundation, and a Holsworth Research Endowment. I am supported by an ELEVATE Boosting Women in STEM Scholarship and was a Global Change Scholar with UQ and CSIRO (2023-2024).

Before moving to Australia for my undergraduate studies in 2017, I trained and worked as a PADI Divemaster in Tanzania (which is where I grew up). I went on to study at USyd and UQ, completing a First-Class Honours project on using DNA metabarcoding to characterise the distribution of planktonic echinoderm larvae, and graduating with the University Medal as Faculty Graduate of the Year.

Following my undergraduate studies, I worked as a full-time research assistant with the Riginos Research Group (2021-2023), primarily leading eDNA sample collection and laboratory processing, bioinformatics, and stakeholder engagement for the Sustainable Urban Seascapes Moreton Bay (Quandamooka) project. I also collaborated with and led laboratory assays for researchers in the Marine Spatial Ecology Lab, the Tibbetts Lab and Dr. Ordonez Alvarez at USC. My work combines population and conservation genomics, evolutionary genomics and environmental DNA to connect patterns of genetic variation to the environments and evolutionary processes that produce them.

I am an active science communicator and a long-standing volunteer with Reef Check Australia, working to bring science to schools, community groups and the public.
